title: Narrative drives startups
date: 2016-05-11 08:50 EDT
tags:
---
For a long time I thought building the best possible product was the most important goal for a startup.  I was wrong.

The thing a startup cannot live without is a strong narrative.  The narrative is what investors pay attention to.  The narrative is what brings in the money.  Raising money attracts attention.

The narrative is usually about why the startup is special.  It's the reason to pay attention despite knowing nothing about what the startup does, what it will make and how it will improve your life.  The narrative is a story first for investors and later becomes talking points for the media.

The narrative is usually about the people involved, their credentials and the parties that legitimize them.  Legitimacy comes in the form of board members, venture capital firms, colleges, past employers and obviously actual success.

There are four major types of narratives:

 * College drop-out genius
 * Team of MIT grads
 * Former Google/Facebook/Microsoft engineers
 * Former successful founder(s) doing it again

A startup is like the plot of a movie.  These are the main characters.  Major producers are attached and the budget is huge.  Everyone expects this one to be a blockbuster.

The narrative is the product.  Everything else is secondary.

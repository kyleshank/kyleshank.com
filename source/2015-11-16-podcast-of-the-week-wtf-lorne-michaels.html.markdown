title:  "Podcast of the week: WTF"
date:   2015-11-16 23:45:00
---
The best podcast of the past week was easy:  [Marc Maron finally got to interview Lorne Michaels in a rare extended interview](http://www.wtfpod.com/podcast/episodes/episode_653_-_lorne_michaels).

Lorne is a bit of a mythical figure of comedy and it was fascinating to hear his thinking behind Saturday Night Live.  As someone who reads comedy biographies and books on SNL I was clearly already on board.  

What I found most interesting though is to hear how Lorne put together a process that worked (more or less) for 40 years.  After reading [Built to Last](http://www.amazon.com/Built-Last-Successful-Visionary-Essentials/dp/0060516402), I think a lot about the concept of being a clock-maker instead of a time-teller.  Lorne built a clock that has continued to tick through several generations of cast members and a changing nation.

SNL is an institution and there is really nothing else like it.  It's not for everyone throughout their lives and Lorne seemed to understand that.  He knows that for many people their favorite cast is probably the one they watched in high school.  Lorne's right: mine was.

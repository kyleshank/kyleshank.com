title: Marketing driven product decisions
date: 2016-05-05 08:26 EDT

---
Silicon Valley on HBO gets a lot right about what its like to work in a startup.  Last weeks episode nailed what it feels like to be an engineer that gets railroaded by marketing driven product decisions.

The thing they got so right about it was just how fast and unilaterally these kinds of things happen.  You can walk in one day and all of a sudden you're working on an entirely different product.  The arguments for the changes are usually paper thin and based on someone's hunch.  There is no discussion: this is what we are doing now.  Doing something right or at quality gets traded for speed and risk.

If the benefits from these marketing driver product decisions actually yielded results I wouldn't have such a problem with them.  My experience hasn't shown that to be true.  The half baked ideas are rushed to production and no real impact is made.  No data backed the decision in the first place so conveniently there is usually no plan or desire to actually measure the outcome.  The lack of follow-up to validate the original decision sets the precendent for many more marketing driven product decisions to come.

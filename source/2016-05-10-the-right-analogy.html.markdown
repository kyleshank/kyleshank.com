title: The Right Analogy

---
Sometimes when you're building a product finding the right analogy can help answer a lot of questions.  I'm building out FANS.FM and have struggled with how to position the product.  What should FANS.FM look and feel like to draw out correct assumptions from new users?  

When I started this latest iteration I thought my analogy was a YouTube for podcasts.  I wanted to build a place where people could publish podcasts for free and also have an easy way to consume them too.  The goal was for FANS.FM to be a two way street: a place where you can both be a creator and a listener.

The YouTube analogy breaks down when it comes to the concept of ownership. I want podcast creators to feel ownership over their shows.  Many of the features I'm building are centered around audience and membership.

The other hard truth is that the only real users of FANS.FM will be podcast creators for a long time.  While building a two-way street is nice it will be one-way traffic for awhile.  All of my ideas around discovery and distribution will only make sense once there is enough happening on FANS.FM to be worth exploring.

Then it hit me: the right analogy is Tumblr.  Tumblr's interface is geared mostly toward the creators but also satisfies the consumers.  Creators have ownership in the form of a subdomain and custom domain if they choose. They can theme their sites and make them their own.  At the same time Tumblr users can also be consumers of the platform by following and liking and snarking and being precious.

tl;dr FANS.FM should be the Tumblr of podcasts.
